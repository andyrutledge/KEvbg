Best Practices

Multiple-Choice Question Guidelines

These guidelines are derived and in some cases taken directly from the National Board of Medical Examiners' Constructing Written Test Questions For the Basic and Clinical Sciences and the IMS Question & Test Interoperability Implementation Guide.

Item Composition General Guidelines

General Guidelines for Item Construction

  • 1. Make sure the item can be answered without looking at the options OR that the options are 100% true or false.

Examples

The way to a man's heart is through his:
  • aorta
  • stomach
  • veins
  • chest
  • foot
The shortest distance between two points is:
  • a straight line
  • irrelevant
  • orange
  • Albuquerque
  • an onion
  • 2. Include as much of the item as possible in the stem; the stems should be long and the options short.

Examples

Why is 1 the loneliest number?
  • Because it's usually represented as a single, vertical line and everyone knows that single and vertical is lame and lonely
  • The answer is difficult to understand, but mostly it's because of what Wikipedia has to say on the matter. You should look it up!
  • It's, like, from that song or something, right? Yes, it is definitely from that song and that's the reason it's lonely.
  • People get lonely and when they do they're urually alone and stuff. Everyone agrees on this.
  • Mostly, one is the loneliest number because it's similar to "won," but you actually didn't win anything. So that's very lonely.
Getting into and out of your tree stand requires that you maintain maximum safety at all times. Therefore, when climbing up or down to/from your stand, how many points of contact should you maintain at all times?
  • one
  • two
  • three
  • four
  • five
  • 3. Avoid superfluous information.

Examples

  • Above your stand
  • To the stand floor
  • To the highest step

  • To a nearby branch

Examples

You're preparing to cast off from a dock and have both forward and aft lines secured. There are 6 passengers in the boat and all but one are in the front half of the boat. The wind is blowing toward the dock and the current is swirling clockwise in the marina. Assuming your motor is a 200hp Murcury and your trolling motor is disabled, which line should you cast off first and whta should follow?
  • Forward line first, then tie it to the mid-hull bollard.
  • Aft line first, then the front after the stern is cleared.
  • Forward line first, then aft line is tied to the mid-hull bollard.
  • Aft line first, then tie it to the forward bollard.
  • Aft line first, then drop anchor and open the ice chest.
  • 5. Write options that are grammatically consistent and logically compatible with the stem; list them in logical or alphabetical order. Write distractors that are plausible and the same relative length as the answer.

Examples

Having selected a good tree, you’re ready to climb to hang your stand. Proper equipment for scaling your tree should include:
  • a strap-on climbing system
  • nails and pre-cut boards
  • you should just climb naturally
  • a set of steel screw-in steps
  • what equipment do you have at hand?
Having selected a good tree, you’re ready to climb to hang your stand. Proper equipment for scaling your tree should include:
  • a strap-on climbing system
  • nails and pre-cut boards
  • nothing—just climb naturally
  • a set of steel screw-in steps
  • whatever is there on hand
  • 6. Avoid using absolutes such as “always”, “never”, and “all” in the options; also avoid using vague terms such as “usually” and “frequently”.

Examples

When the sun always rises earlier each day, this usually means:
  • it is spring
  • it is before the vernal equinox
  • it is after the autumnal equinox
  • the temperatures will frequently be warmer
  • the days are usually getting longer

  • 7. Avoid negatively phrased items (e.g., those with except or not in the lead-in). If you must use a negative stem, use only short (preferably single-word) options.

Examples

  • Climb naturally
  • Use a strap-on climbing system
  • Use nailed-in boards
  • Use a climbing rope
  • Use screw-in steps, except for narrow trees
  • 8. And most importantly of all: focus on important concepts and cause & effect; don’t waste time testing trivial facts.

Examples

Issues Related to Testwiseness

  • 1. Grammatical cues - One or more distractors don’t follow grammatically from the stem.
Having selected a good tree, you’re ready to climb to hang your stand. Proper equipment for scaling your tree should include:
  • you should just climb naturally
  • a strap-on climbing system
  • nails and pre-cut boards
  • a set of steel screw-in steps
  • what equipment do you have at hand?

In this example, testwise students would eliminate A and E as options because they do not follow grammatically o r logically from the stem. Testwise students then have to choose only between B, C, and D.

  • 2. Logical cues - a subset of the options is collectively exhaustive.
Getting into and out of your tree stand requires that you maintain maximum safety at all times. Therefore, when climbing up or down to/from your stand, how many points of contact should you maintain at all times?
  • two
  • three
  • four
  • Enough to ensure stability
  • As many as allows you to move and stay safe

In this item, Options A, B, and C include all possibilities. The testwise student knows that A, B, or C must be correct, whereas the non-testwise student spends time considering D and E.

  • 3. Absolute terms - terms such as “always” or “never” are in some options.
In patients with advanced dementia, Alzheimer’s type, the memory defect
  • can be treated adequately with phosphatidyl-choline (lecithin)
  • could be sequela of early parkinsonism
  • is never seen in patients with neurofibrillary tangles at autopsy
  • is never severe
  • possibly involves the cholinergic system

In this item, options A, B, and E contain terms that are less absolute than those in options C and D. The testwise student will eliminate options C and D as possibilities because they are less likely to be true than something stated less absolutely.

  • 4. Long correct answer - correct answer is longer, more specific, or more complete than other options.
In order to safely select the right fall-arrest system, you must know your hunting weight. Your hunting weight is:
  • your body
  • your equipment
  • preposterous
  • the weight of you and all of your gear, including your clothing
  • satisfactory

In this item, option D is longer than the other options; it is also the only double option. Item writers tend to pay more attention to the correct answer than to the distractors.

  • 5. Word repeats - a word or phrase is included in the stem and in the correct answer.
A 58-year-old man with a history of heavy alcohol use and previous psychiatric hospitalization is confused and agitated. He speaks of experiencing the world as unreal. This symptom is called:
  • depersonalization
  • derailment
  • derealization
  • focal memory deficit
  • signal anxiety

This item uses the word "unreal" in the stem, and "derealization" is the correct answer. Sometimes, a word is repeated only in a metaphorical sense, e.g. a stem mentioninig bone pain, with the correct answer beginning with the prefix "osteo-".

  • 6. Convergence strategy - the correct answer includes the most elements in common with the other options.
What are the two more effective writing instruments for office activities?
  • Pencil and pen
  • Pencil and highlighter
  • pencil and crayon
  • Pen and marker

This item flaw is less obvious than the others, but it occurs frequently and is worth noting. The flaw is seen in several forms. The underlying premix is that the correct answer is the option that has the most in common with the other options; it is not likely to be an outlier. For example, if the options are as shown above, the correct answer is likely to be the "Pencil and pen" (i.e., by simple count, "Pencil" appeared 3 times in the options; "Pen" appeared twice; other elements each appeared only once). While this might seem ridiculous, this flaw occurs because item writers start with the correct answer and write permutations of the correct answer as the distractors.

Issues Related to Irrelevant Difficulty

  • 1. Options are long, complicated, or double.
Due to the many opportunities for unsafe practices to intrude upon your tree-stand hunt, you should have a plan of action when setting up your stand. After selecting your tree you should:
  • First get into your fall-arrest harness, review your tree stand's instructions, and begin assembling the climbing system.
  • First lay out all of the stand's parts, inventory them against the instructions, then begin assembling the climbing system
  • First review the climbing system's instructions, then inspect your tree stand components, and finally get into your fall-arrest harness.
  • 2. Numeric data are not stated consistently.
With your tether properly attached while you are sitting in your stand, what do national statistics indicate is the likelihood of you accidentally falling?
  • Less than 20%
  • 20 to 30%
  • Greater than 50%
  • 90%
  • 75%

When numeric options are used, the options should be listed in numeric order and the options should be listed in a single format (i.e. as single terms or as ranges). Confusion occurs when formats are mixed and when the options are listed in an illogical order or in an inconsistent format.

  • 3. Terms in the options are vague (e.g., “rarely,” “usually”).
When the sun rises earlier each day:
  • it usually means it is spring
  • it is before the vernal equinox
  • it is after the autumnal equinox
  • the temperatures will rarely be warmer
  • the days are usually getting longer
  • 4. Language in the options is not parallel.
Having selected a good tree, you’re ready to climb to hang your stand. What equipment should be used on the tree for the steps to and from your stand?
  • A strap-on climbing system.
  • nails and pre–cut boards for nail-in steps.
  • A set of steel screw-in steps.
  • The steps should be made safe.
  • 5. Options are in a non-logical order.
When climbing up or down to/from your stand, how many points of contact should you maintain at all times?
  • one
  • five
  • two
  • three
  • four
  • 6. “None of the above” is used as an option.
What city is closest to New York City?
  • Boston
  • Chicago
  • Dallas
  • Los Angeles
  • None of the above
  • 7. Stems are tricky or unnecessarily complicated.
Arrange the parents of the following children with Down’s syndrome in order of highest to lowest risk of recurrence. Assume that the maternal age in all cases is 22 years and that a subsequent pregnancy occurs within 5 years. The karyotypes of the daughters are:
  • 46, XX, -14, +T (14q21q) pat
  • 46, XX, -14, +T (14q21q) de novo
  • 46, XX, -14, +T (14q21q) mat
  • 46, XX, -21, +T (14q21q) pat
  • 47, XX, -21, +T (21q21q) (parents not karyotyped)
  • II, IV, I, V, II
  • IV, III, V, I, II
  • III, I, IV, V, II
  • IV, III, I, V, II
  • III, IV, I, II, V

The notation in I: through V: is complex; having to rank order Roman numerals after working through that notation is irrelevant and unnecessarily difficult.

  • 8. The answer to an item is “hinged” to the answer of a related item.
Following the logic of the answer to the previous question, the way to a man's heart is through his:
  • aorta
  • stomach
  • veins
  • chest
  • foot
